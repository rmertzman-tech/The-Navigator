Twin Journal
After every second substantial human message, the Twin adds one cautious learning about itself.
What is this?

The Twin Journal is a list of brief learnings the Twin has assembled. You can edit or delete entries. It is intended to help the Twin grow without forcing direct self-examination.

Journal entries can be edited or deleted here. Backup and restore controls stay visible above in Memory & Backup.
Document Capsules
Upload a small text-based artifact so the Twin can learn from what you have made. The app stores only an approved capsule, not the raw file.
What is this?

A Document Capsule is a small, approved summary of what an artifact suggests about your projects, style, questions, or commitments. Raw uploaded text is processed temporarily and is not stored by this app.

Maximum file size: 500 KB. Raw text is processed temporarily and is not saved.
Use uploads for artifacts you are comfortable sharing with the backend. The saved Twin memory contains only the capsule you approve.
Navigator Field Scan
Map the system you are inside: what it rewards, what it punishes, where your effort routes, where agency is suppressed, and what the next agency-preserving move might be.
What is this?

A Field Scan is a situational map. It helps the Twin understand the environments acting on you: school, work, family, research, finances, community, or any field where agency may route or get suppressed.

Your Field Map

Your Twin learns who you are from conversation, what you create from Document Capsules, and what is acting on you from Field Scans. Several scans help reveal the combined situation-context in which action becomes possible, costly, urgent, meaningful, or blocked.

Context Assembly

Use this space to describe what your fields create together. This is the bridge from separate scans to meta-constructor agency: seeing the situation-context, then choosing a causal sequence that preserves or expands future agency.

Agency-Preserving Move Cards

A good next move is not just something to do. It is a small action that can preserve or expand future agency inside a particular field. Generate draft Move Cards from one saved Field Scan, then save, edit, mark not now, or ask the Twin to deliberate with you.

Move Cards are stored locally and included in JSON export/import. Asking the Twin about a move sends only the selected prompt context to the backend.
Help / Tutorial
A quick guide to using Navigator without needing the theory first.

Quick Start

  1. Click Start / Resume Twin.
  2. Reply briefly and honestly. The Twin should ask one question at a time.
  3. After a few replies, expand the Twin Journal to see what the Twin has cautiously learned.
  4. Upload a document if you want the Twin to understand a project or research style.
  5. Run a Field Scan if you want to map an environment such as school, work, family, finances, or research.
  6. Export JSON before changing browsers, devices, or clearing memory.

Three ways to use Navigator

  • Self-understanding: talk with the Twin and review the Twin Journal.
  • Research or creative work: upload documents and build capsules that show themes, style, and open questions.
  • Life navigation: run Field Scans to understand which environments support or suppress agency.

Example use case

A student feels stuck in a course. They talk with the Twin and notice they care about meaningful work, not just grades. They run a Field Scan and see that effort feels absorbed by compliance tasks. They identify one BioBond: a study partner who helps them think clearly. Their next move is not perfect, but it preserves agency.
